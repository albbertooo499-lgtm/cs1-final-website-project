JACK THE RIPPER: THE BIG QUESTION

Jack the ripper (Jack the Ripper) It's the nickname given to the serial killer not identificated who terrorized the Whitechapel district of London in late 1888. His identity remains one of the most famous and unsolved criminal enigmas in history..

the person behind the name

  • nickname: The name "Jack the Ripper" was not a police name, but was popularized through the press, after a letter (possibly fake) arrived at a news agency signed with that name..
  • Attributed Skills: The nature of the mutilations on his canonical victims (the five women officially attributed to him) suggested that the killer possessed some anatomical or surgical knowledge.
  • who was supposed to be:Researchers at the time suggested that he might have been a man who could blend in easily, perhaps with a profession that gave him access to sharp instruments and knowledge of the human body (doctor, butcher, etc.). ).

suspects according to their identity

suspects according to their identity:

  • Aaron Kosminski: A Polish barber who lived in Whitechapel. He was a key suspect for contemporary police and in recent decades has been the subject of controversial DNA analysis pointing to him.
  • Montague John Druitt: An upper-class lawyer whose name surfaced in late police reports. His suicide in December 1888 coincided with the cessation of crimes, making him a strong suspect.
  • Francis Tumblety: An American "pseudo-doctor" with a deep hatred of women. He fled the country in November 1888. He was considered a prime suspect by the chief inspector of the time.

    THE TERROR FINAL

    The murders stopped as abruptly as they began after November 1888. Theories about this cessation include:

    • the ripper died (as was possibly the case with Druitt).
    • was imprisoned for a minor offense (as was possibly the case with Kosminski, who was placed in an asylum).
    • him ran away from London or the country (like Tumblety).

    All of this information is not known for certain; the identity of Jack the Ripper remains a great mystery..
